What I am all about.

Hello everyone,
my name is Fernando Mateus de Oliveira, Brazilian, born in João Pessoa, Paraíba. I am a software developer and here I described a little bit of my journey since I had the first contact with Computer Science.

I have started my career when I still was a Computer Science student at Federal University of Paraiba - UFPB Campus IV. In that journey, my first contact with the project was related to Computer Science Education on PIBID project, managed by Pasqueline Dantas. There, I learned about the K-12 Curriculum and executed some practices activities to evaluate many aspects in the student life of a secondary school.

After PIBID, I had an opportunity to get hired on my first internship. It became myself a Java developer at SIM Project, a project to rebuild all the software modules using a most effective and enterprise technology of the João Pessoa's City Hall. There, I worked with Java, JBoss Seam, JSF and PostgreSQL as a database. In that journey, I was too excited that on my vacation I worked on a full-time journey because I was learning a lot with my coworkers. I really felt that I was a member of that team.

Another opportunity to work as an intern was at Pacto Tecnologia where a worked with Java, EJB, JSF and MySQL as a database to a project based on the data of this company to recommend the sales department to contact the customer and offer better services and products. In parallel, I took tasks to maintain the legacy systems related to Farmacia Popular, a government social program, based on a web service to allow pharmacies deliver free drugs to patients. There, I had my first contact with cloud computing technologies and participate of acquirement of the MPS.BR level F maturity model of quality.

I was too excited about those opportunities during my degree program, working as a software developer. Towards, I was studying a lot of technologies and dedicating all my free time to it, but there was a little feeling inside me indicating that I must learn to acquire other skills during my graduation like to have a more effective communication and related to academic researches. So, I was involved with another project inside the faculty. I was monitory to the introductory class of algorithm and programming.

In that way mentioned above, I worked as a scholarship in the PROLICEN project managed by Ayla Rebouças, a professor which I dedicate all my respect. It was a journey of a huge learning about education, software development and how to be a better person. We built a software to help teachers to detect learning difficulties by students. After, these requirements were used to evaluate a framework purposed by Rodrigo Vilar with my collaboration. Ayla also was my counsellor on my monography.

According to my expertise acquired before, I got my first opportunity as an effective software developer at Ellos Connecti. I worked there for 2 years and 7 months. Our team developed a software to store documents on the cloud-related solution, which need to be stored in a minimum of 4 years and has been easy to access when will occur and audit process. I had experiences with Java, Spring Roo, JSF and Swing, MySQL, AWS Services.

After my degree, I had an opportunity to work at one of the most respected laboratories of software development of the Paraíba belonging of Federal University of Campina Grande. I worked in three different projects on Virtus, UFCG.
In my 1st project, I was coordinated by Rodrigo Vilar, the same professor that offered an opportunity to collaborate of his Ph.D. job. There, I worked with a very dedicated team and very focused on accomplishing the task and maintain a good environment. Our team was subdivided for 2 little teams. I was in the team which built an App to run on the Windows 10. Our app was developed using C# and UWP framework. Also with the same team, my 2nd project was a game based on Mahjong strategies to old-aged people. We built this game using Unity 3D and C#, and Photon Multiplayer Framework and App42 Cloud APIs.
In the 3rd project, I had a lot of challenges. The context was focused on build evidence by reports and insights using the big data of our customer. I was allowed to nomited myselft as an external member of the HP Enterprise Analytics team. It was my first contact with Data Analysis. We used a platform called Databrics, Python and Spark to deliver our results.

Living Object Model - LOM Framework

Living Object Model developed by Rodrigo Vilar and my colaboration. It is an implementation of AOM pattern to build adaptable and adaptive systems. Each layer of this framework is versioned in diferents repositories like described bellow. This design was based on a TDD approach based on AOM architectural pattern.

LOM-api

Domain classes

LOM-business

Business layer

LOM-memory-dao

Persistence layer in memory

LOM-mysql-dao

Persistence with Neo4J

LOM-old

LOM initial repository
